① Area–Mach Relation (quasi-1-D isentropic)
A/A* = (1/M)·[(2/(γ+1))·(1+(γ-1)/2·M²)]^((γ+1)/(2(γ-1)))
Derived from ρVA = const + energy eq. Differentiating gives:
dA/A = (M²-1)·dV/V → throat (dA=0) ⟹ M=1 or dV=0

② Isentropic Flow Relations (adiabatic + reversible)
T₀/T = 1 + (γ-1)/2·M²
P₀/P = [1+(γ-1)/2·M²]^(γ/(γ-1))
ρ₀/ρ = [1+(γ-1)/2·M²]^(1/(γ-1))
V = M·a = M·√(γRT)

③ Design Mach Number
Solve A/A* = AR_exit for the supersonic (M>1) root numerically.
For air (γ=1.4): AR=3 → M≈2.637; AR=4 → M≈2.940

④ Four Flow Regimes
• P_b > P_b,sub: Subsonic unchoked (M<1 everywhere)
• P_b,shock < P_b < P_b,sub: Normal shock in diverging section
• P_b,design < P_b < P_b,shock: Over-expanded (external oblique shock)
• P_b ≤ P_b,design: Fully isentropic supersonic (design point)

⑤ Critical Back-Pressures
P_b,sub = P₀/[1+(γ-1)/2·M_sub²]^(γ/(γ-1)) (subsonic exit)
P_b,design = P₀/[1+(γ-1)/2·M_sup²]^(γ/(γ-1)) (full supersonic)
P_b,shock@exit = P₀·(P₀₂/P₀₁)·[1+(γ-1)/2·M₂²]^(-γ/(γ-1))

Ref: Anderson, Modern Compressible Flow, §5.1–5.4

① Rankine–Hugoniot Jump Conditions
M₂² = [(γ-1)M₁²+2] / [2γM₁²-(γ-1)]
P₂/P₁ = 1 + 2γ/(γ+1)·(M₁²-1)
T₂/T₁ = [P₂/P₁]·[2+(γ-1)M₁²] / [(γ+1)M₁²]
ρ₂/ρ₁ = (γ+1)M₁² / [2+(γ-1)M₁²]
V₂/V₁ = ρ₁/ρ₂ (mass conservation)

② Stagnation Pressure Ratio
P₀₂/P₀₁ = [P₀(M₂)/P(M₂)] / [P₀(M₁)/P(M₁)]
Always < 1 for M₁ > 1 → entropy production. For M₁=2: P₀₂/P₀₁ ≈ 0.721

③ Entropy Generation
Δs = R·ln(ρ₂/ρ₁) - Cᵥ·ln(P₂/P₁)
Equivalently: Δs/R = −ln(P₀₂/P₀₁) > 0 (2nd Law)

④ Shock Location Physics
Shock moves toward throat as P_b ↑ (higher back pressure).
Shock at exit: P_b = P₀·(P₀₂/P₀₁)/[1+(γ-1)/2·M₂²]^(γ/(γ-1))

⑤ Numerical Check (M₁=2, γ=1.4)
M₂ = 0.5774, P₂/P₁ = 4.500, T₂/T₁ = 1.688, P₀₂/P₀₁ = 0.7209

Ref: Anderson, §3.6–3.9; Shapiro Vol.1 §4

EXP 3 Throat Sonic Choking & Mass Flow Saturation
Objective: Demonstrate the phenomenon of aerodynamic choking — show that once the throat reaches M = 1, further reduction in back pressure cannot increase the mass flow rate. Understand why information cannot propagate upstream past a sonic throat (acoustic barrier). Derive and verify the choked mass flow equation.
Choked Flow Critical Pressure Ratio P*/P₀ Mass Flow Rate ṁ Acoustic Barrier Sonic Conditions at Throat
  1. Set P₀, T₀, throat area A*, and exit area ratio.
  2. Run simulation — a sweep of Pb/P₀ from 0.05 to 1.0 is performed.
  3. Observe the ṁ vs Pb/P₀ curve — note the flat plateau below P*/P₀ ≈ 0.528.
  4. Verify ṁ_max using the choked flow formula with your P₀, T₀, A* values.
  5. Explore how ṁ_max scales with P₀ (linear) and T₀ (inverse √). Complete Lab Tasks.
① Choked Mass Flow Rate
ṁ_max = A*·P₀/√T₀ · √(γ/R) · [2/(γ+1)]^((γ+1)/(2(γ-1)))
Derived from: ṁ = ρ·V·A at throat (M=1) with ρ*,V* from isentropic relations.
For air: ṁ_max ≈ 0.0404·A*·P₀/√T₀ [kg/s, Pa, m², K]

② Critical Pressure Ratio
P*/P₀ = [2/(γ+1)]^(γ/(γ-1)) ≈ 0.5283 for air (γ=1.4)
Once P_b/P₀ ≤ P*/P₀, throat reaches M=1 → flow is choked.
Further P_b reduction does NOT increase ṁ.

③ Critical (Sonic) Conditions at Throat
T* = T₀·2/(γ+1) = 0.8333·T₀ for air
P* = P₀·[2/(γ+1)]^(γ/(γ-1)) = 0.5283·P₀
ρ* = ρ₀·[2/(γ+1)]^(1/(γ-1)) = 0.6339·ρ₀
a* = √(γRT*) = √(γRT₀·2/(γ+1))

④ Acoustic Barrier Argument
Sound speed a = √(γRT). At M=1: flow speed = a → pressure waves cannot
propagate upstream → downstream changes cannot affect ṁ.

⑤ Scaling Laws
ṁ ∝ A*·P₀ (linear in P₀)
ṁ ∝ 1/√T₀ (inverse square-root in T₀)

Ref: Anderson §5.4; NACA TN 1135

① Isentropic Relations (all properties)
T/T₀ = [1+(γ-1)/2·M²]⁻¹
P/P₀ = [1+(γ-1)/2·M²]^(-γ/(γ-1))
ρ/ρ₀ = [1+(γ-1)/2·M²]^(-1/(γ-1))

② Continuity Equation (quasi-1D)
ρ·V·A = ṁ = const
Near throat: as A↓, V↑ and ρ↓ simultaneously (compressibility matters).

F = ṁ·V_exit + (P_exit − P_atm)·A_exit
Momentum term + pressure-area term.

Temperature gradient: smooth (∝ M²); Pressure gradient: steepest in diverging section.

EXP 5 Nozzle Design Optimisation & Thrust Performance
Objective: Determine the optimum exit area ratio that maximises thrust coefficient Cf at different altitudes (sea level, cruise, stratosphere, space). Understand the trade-off between over-expanded and under-expanded nozzle operation. Compare real engine designs (SSME, Merlin 1D, Merlin Vacuum) and explain why different area ratios are used.
Thrust Coefficient C_f Specific Impulse I_sp Over/Under-Expansion Altitude Compensation Rocket Nozzle Design
  1. Set combustion conditions (P₀, T₀) and throat area A*.
  2. Set ambient pressures for four altitudes.
  3. Run simulation — Cf vs AR is plotted for each altitude.
  4. Read the summary table — optimal AR, M_exit, Cf, thrust F, and Isp at each altitude.
  5. Look up real engines and compare their AR with your simulation. Complete Lab Tasks.
① Thrust Equation
F = ṁ·V_exit + (P_exit − P_atm)·A_exit
1st term = momentum thrust; 2nd = pressure thrust (zero at perfect expansion).

② Thrust Coefficient
Cf = F / (P₀·A*) (dimensionless, independent of scale)
Maximised when P_exit = P_atm (perfectly expanded).
Vacuum Cf,max = Cf,vac = Cf,mom + Cf,pressure (can exceed 2 for high AR).

③ Specific Impulse
Isp = F / (ṁ·g₀) [seconds], g₀ = 9.81 m/s²
Proportional to V_exit for perfectly expanded: Isp ≈ V_exit/g₀.

④ Optimum Area Ratio
Solve P_exit(AR) = P_atm for perfect expansion:
P₀/P_atm = [1+(γ-1)/2·M²]^(γ/(γ-1)) → find M_exit → AR = A/A*(M)

⑤ Choked Mass Flow (rocket)
ṁ = A*·P₀·√(γ/(R·T₀))·[2/(γ+1)]^((γ+1)/(2(γ-1)))

⑥ Over/Under-Expansion Loss
Over-expanded (P_exit < P_atm): external oblique shock → C_f penalty.
Under-expanded (P_exit > P_atm): expansion fans outside → wasted thrust.

Ref: Sutton & Biblarz, Rocket Propulsion Elements Ch.3; Anderson §4.5
